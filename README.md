# echo

## what it is
echo is a customized flashcard and audio listening app. it helps me learn specific french and japanese phrases for my daily life. 

## how it works
echo features two distinct learning methods. 
* **flashcards**: this mode hides the target translation to force active recall before revealing the answer. 
* **listen library**: this mode displays all translations immediately and provides an audio button for each phrase. 

the phrases are grouped into categories like morning routines, commutes, exercise, and work shifts at brooklyn boulders. the app integrates with my external audio generators, voix and koe, to supply the audio files. 

## why i created it
generic language apps teach random vocabulary and fail to build functional communication skills. i need to speak with clients at the climbing gym. i created echo to learn the exact sentences i use every day. 

echo applies a three-step language learning system. 

* **personalized sentence lists**: i built lists of complete sentences based on my daily routine. these act as my “language islands”. learning complete sentences teaches grammar naturally through pattern recognition. 
* **audio shadowing**: i use voix and koe to generate clear audio for every custom sentence. i listen to these files repeatedly on the train and practice shadowing by repeating the phrases out loud. this approach trains both listening comprehension and physical pronunciation. 
* **active recall**: the flashcard mode forces my brain to produce the target language from memory. reading the english phrase and struggling to remember the translation builds lasting retention. this mental friction is necessary for achieving real fluency.
